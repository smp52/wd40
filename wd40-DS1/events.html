OFFERINGS

Paddling Information

We hope you enjoy all the paddling options we offer at The Paddling Center. All paddling platforms are highly durable and stable and come with Coast Guard approved Personal Floatation Devices and appropriate equipment for the given platform. Both paddling and basic safety instructions are given prior to launching so no paddling experience is necessary.

Tour guides are available but only via advanced reservation of at least 24 hours (sometimes up to a week or more) for larger groups or specialty groups (e.g., corporate team building). There is a 2 hour minimum for both kayak and canoe rentals.

Available paddle platforms

10 RTM Brio 12.75ft kayak (tandem)

10 Emotion Advant-edge 12ft touring kayaks (singles)

10 Aluminum Grumman 15ft canoes

5 Paddle Boards: Jimmy Styks, model Orca (11ft, soft body)

Tour Information

Our vision and goal for each one of our tours is to blend information about eco, culture, and history into an entertaining narrative that is spiced with a little humor. We strive to allow people to enjoy Shingle Creek in a safe and leisurely fashion all while learning something new and interesting.

Duffy Electric Boat Ride

Our electric boat may carry up to eight guests for tours lasting between 45-50 minutes. Tours run north of The Paddling Center for up to 1.5 miles and (if Creek conditions allow) South of the Paddling Center up to 1.5 miles. The boat captain will provide edu-tainment narrative that covers local history/lore and ecological facts about the Creek and surrounding flora, fauna, and wildlife. Tours may be only edu-tainment oriented (fun and interesting, not too deep), or may have a special flavor, such as picture taking, bird watching, etc. We also may have opportunities to include individuals (local ecologist, graduate biology/environmental science students, etc.) to offer more in-depth and focused narrative. Our Duffy boat runs silently through the water at reduced speeds (6mph and below) and will slow or stop for picture taking, wildlife viewing, etc. as needed.

Kayak/Canoe Tours

At The Paddling Center we offer both 2 hour guided tours of Shingle Creek and 3-4 hour guided training tours to Makinson Island. In order to maximize the enjoyment of each of our guests, we do limit the number of persons and boats that each guide/instructor can accompany on the water. Our maximum ratios are listed below.

Adults - 12 people per guide / 6 boats per guide

Kids - 10 people per guide / 5 boats per guide

The quantities above reflect the maximum number of persons and boats that each guide may accompany.Adult Boats are classified as any boat (canoe or kayak) with at least one adult age 15 and older. If you do not wish to hire enough guides to cover the number of people or boats in your group, you may designate a portion of your group to be under the purview of the guide. The other members of your group may not remain in the vicinity of the guide.

Excursion Information

Group Events & BBQ

The Paddling Center has several on-site picnic tables for hosting group events, such as birthday parties, family reunions, team building, etc. There is a stainless steel gas grill available to rent ($10/hr) unless you prefer to bring your own. Be sure to call and reserve the tables in advance. We offer group discount for multi-equipment multi-hour rentals. Having hiking and biking trails adjacent to the Paddling Center facility allows for a range of outdoor activities. What a great way to spend an afternoon or a day!

Biking

Boys, girls, and children single speed bikes for rent by the hour. All bike rentals include attached basket, chain and lock, and safety helmet.

Camping

Camping packets available, that will include 4 man tent, sleeping bags and ground mats. Additional basic equipment may be made available such as small gas stove for cooking.

School Field Trips/Edu-tainment Opportunities

Our overall vision and goal to blend information about eco, culture, history, etc. into an entertaining narrative that is spiced with a little humor. The goal is allow people to enjoy Creek in a safe and leisure fashion and realize at the end they maybe learned something new and interesting.

For elementary and middle school classes, we offer a truly unique, interesting and fun field trip experience that incorporates learning the skills needed to safely paddle a canoe (i.e., teamwork, communication, safety checks, paddling techniques, etc.) with nature-based exercises and games. Classes include Birds in Flight, Predator-Prey Relationships, Eco-Discovery (using canoes or the 18ft electric Duffy boat), and assorted cooperative game-based exercises. The goal is to provide a relevant eco-based curricula with on-the-water skill building that is fun and challenging.

We also are working in collaboration with the Osceola County Welcome Center and History Museum located directly across Hwy 192, that will allow groups the option to tour the History Museum as part of their pre-established curricula. Registration/Cancellation Policy

Prepayment in full is required for all reservations (Grade 3-12 school groups and camps must pre-pay for guides, catering orders, and boat deliveries, but may pay for rented boats on the day of the event). Reservations must be cancelled by 5pm the night before the event (except deliveries, catering orders, and off-site guiding/instruction). Cancellations due to weather must be made at least 2 hours before the event. Small reductions in boat quantities for non-peak-hour reservations are accepted when you arrive for your event, but are subject to the 10% fee. For all cancellations made after the above deadlines, no refunds or credits will be issued. Reservations begin at the designated start time, and groups arriving late will not receive refunds or credits for missed time.
